Prawn Crackers

Prawn crackers are tasty prawn-flavored crisps that puff up when deep-fried. Prawn crackers are very addictive despite the fact that their texture has been compared to Styrofoam. They make a great snack food for kids and adults alike and go very well with beer.

Prep Time - Less than 10 Mins

Cooking Time - Less than 1 Min

Serves - Depends how many you fry!

Ingredients:

  • A packet of uncooked Prawn Crackers
  • Oil for Frying

Equipment:

  • A wok, or other frying equipment
  • Wooden Chopstick, or Deep Fry Thermometer
  • Strainer
  • Paper Towels

Method:

  1. Heat the oil to 182C to deep-fry the prawn chips. To find out if the oil is hot enough either use a deep-fry thermometer or place a cooking chopstick or a wooden spoon upright in the middle of the wok (as shown in the photo). If bubbles start forming around the chopstick or wooden spoon immediately, the oil is ready.
  2. Carefully place 5 to 6 chips in the hot oil, separating them if needed. Adding more than that can overcrowd the wok and lower the oil temperature, making the crackers taste greasy.
  3. Deep-fry the chips for 5 seconds or less (until they puff up).
  4. Remove the prawn crackers from the wokâ€”it's easiest to do this quickly if you have a strainer. Drain the prawn crackers on paper towels. Use a fresh paper towel to drain each batch of chips.
  5. Prawn crackers taste best served warm. If not serving immediately, store in a sealed container and briefly reheat in the oven before serving. Peanut sauce is the traditional accompaniment for prawn crackers. What to drink with prawn crackers? Beer, of course, or perhaps a Johannesburg Reisling.
